This was my first project at the Institute of Public Policy and Social Research. I constructed a state-year panel dataset capturing transgender non-discrimination policies across employment, housing, education, insurance, public accommodations, and state employment. 

In addition, the dataset includes measures of anti-equality policies, such as Religious Freedom Restoration Acts, restrictions on municipal non-discrimination protections, and legal protections for medical professionals who decline to provide services to LGBTQ+ individuals. The years range from 2000 to 2024. 

The data were compiled from the Human Rights Campaign's [state equality index](https://www.hrc.org/resources/equality-from-state-to-state) and the Movement Advancement Project [policy maps.](https://www.lgbtmap.org/equality-maps) 

After constructing the dataset, I wrote a blog post for IPPSR's Policy Wonk. It focuses on the importance of these non-discrimination laws in the face of rising anti-trans rhetoric. The post can be found [here.](https://ippsr.msu.edu/public-policy/michigan-wonk-blog/state-legislatures-actions-face-rising-anti-trans-rhetoric)
